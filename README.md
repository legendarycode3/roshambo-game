Roshambo (ROCK PAPER SCISSOR): </br>
A Roshambo (or Rock, Paper, Scissors) is a simple, two-player, zero-sum hand game used
for decision-making or competition. Players simultaneously form one of three shapes
with their hands—rock (fist), paper (flat hand), or scissors (v-shape)—after counting "1, 2, 3, shoot" or "Roshambo".


**Rules and Mechanics** </br>
1. Winning Conditions: Rock crushes scissors, scissors cuts paper, and paper covers rock. </br>
2. Gameplay: If both players show the same sign, it is a tie and the round is replayed. </br>
3. Best of Three: The first player to win two rounds typically wins the game. </br>
4. Hand Signals:  Signals are generally delivered on the final word, often with a shaking motion during the count. </br>
